Together We Belong

Introduction

Answering the “Where people meet” brief, I create community spaces of the future where people are connected, places are healthy, and a good life can be led by a certain group of people.

I designed a space for international students in London. As a sustainable and environmentally friendly place, I expect to make my space within existing galleries in London.

In an era of increasing globalization and cross-cultural interaction, many international students still face challenges in identity and cultural belonging. This sense of loss and loneliness often stems from an inability to find cultural resonance in an unfamiliar environment, as well as from the lack of a safe space to freely express oneself and explore each other’s cultures.

The project provides a platform for cross-cultural exchange between international students, immigrants, and members of the local community. This space is not only a place to socialize, but also a place to build a sense of belonging and identity through shared activities and cultural experiences.

My goal is to help each participant find a sense of belonging in their cultural exploration and to support them in building deeper confidence and understanding in their own identity and cultural identity.

Project ideas

Inter-cultural space design: combining architecture and interior design to create an inclusive and versatile space that allows for cultural workshops, exhibitions, language exchange courses and more.

I wanted to bring in many interactive activities. For example: cooking food together, music, language, cultural games, crafts and festivals held to strengthen community connection through interaction.

Storytelling

I chose this theme because it stems from my personal experience. When I first arrived in London, I felt lost, isolated, and overwhelmed. I longed for a space or a community that could support me in adapting to my new environment, integrating into the local culture, and forming meaningful friendships.

This space is designed to be continuous and ever-evolving, passed down from one group of students to the next. As I move on next year, new members will take my place, and years later, I can return to share my journey.

While people may come and go, the impact of the space extends beyond its immediate community. As members gain confidence and adapt, they will naturally start connecting with other students and engaging with the wider local community, fostering deeper cultural exchange and lasting relationships.
