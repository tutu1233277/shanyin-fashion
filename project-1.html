This project develops a participatory dream drawing workshop that helps people externalise dream experience through images, colour, and atmosphere, without relying on fixed interpretation.

It draws on automatism to make marks with reduced conscious control, so that dream fragments can surface quickly as visual material rather than a fully explained story.

Grounded in Jungian ideas that dreams are symbolic self-portrayals and can serve a compensatory function, the workshop treats the drawing as unconscious material.

By using drawing as a starting point, participants can begin to untangle their dreams and the confusion they may carry about them. Through gentle reflective questions, they are encouraged to consider whether the process helped them notice an aspect of themselves they had not seen before, thereby raising self-awareness.

Inviting professional guidance in the future, further analysis of the participants' paintings can be conducted and used for the workshop, bringing the workshop into actual practice.
