The Comfort Lab

Award: Bronze Award of Asian Vanguared Art & Design Award for "The Comfort Lab: Exploring Furniture, Space, and Body"

In response to the increasing mental and physical strain caused by fast-paced urban life and sedentary work patterns, this project explores how multi-sensory design can create spaces and furniture that promote comfort, relaxation, and embodied well-being.

Inspired by my personal experience with spinal scoliosis, vertebral misalignment, and the discomfort caused by prolonged sitting, the project questions the current standards of everyday furniture and proposes an alternative therapeutic environment for the body and mind.

Drawing on my long-term practice of yoga and meditation, I designed interactive furniture pieces including Flex Seat, a set of yoga pose chairs that encourage restorative postures, and Sense Seat, sensor-based ergonomic chairs embedded with pressure sensors at key points of muscular contact.

These sensors provide real-time feedback on posture and sitting habits, gently guiding users toward more comfortable and supportive positions.

The project also extends comfort beyond furniture through immersive sensory elements, including calming ASMR audio, audio-visualisations projected across the four walls, and a bio-tactile carpet with varied textures that evoke a gentle, embracing touch.
