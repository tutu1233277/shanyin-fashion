Work 02

Dream Archive

Award: Bronze Award of Asian Vanguared Art & Design Award for “The Comfort Lab: Exploring Furniture, Space, and Body” Contextual and Theoretical Studies 3, Industry Practice, Major Project

Project ideas

In response to building a bodily comfort space, and to the increasing mental and physical strain caused by fast-paced urban life and sedentary work patterns, my project explores the potential of multi-sensory design to create spaces and furniture that promote comfort, relaxation, and embodied wellbeing.

I proposed alternatives to current standards and realities of our furniture. Drawing from personal experience with spinal discomfort, yoga and meditation practice, I propose a spatial intervention that integrates interactive furniture, sensor-based ergonomic seating, and immersive sensory elements including sound, touch, and visual stimuli.

The space acts as a therapeutic environment, gently guiding users to re-establish bodily awareness through immersive, multi-sensory engagement.

Research questions

How can multi-sensory interaction design in furniture and spatial environments be used to promote physical comfort and mental relaxation for users affected by sedentary lifestyles and bodily discomfort?

Response

In contemporary society, increasing numbers of people, particularly the younger generation, are overwhelmed by the pressures of fast-paced work. Prolonged sitting and sedentary lifestyles have become a common and often overlooked issue.

Inspired by my personal experience as someone who suffers from spinal scoliosis and vertebral misalignment, I have experienced persistent discomfort and pain due to extended periods of sitting. This led me to investigate how multi-sensory design can contribute to both physical and mental relief.

I aim to create an immersive comfort space supported by a series of interactive furniture pieces, an environment designed to address bodily and mental strains.

Drawing from my long-term practice of yoga and meditation, I recognize the unique capacity of certain postures and movements to alleviate both physical tension and mental fatigue. I designed a series of “yoga pose chair”, called “Flex Seat”.

I also incorporated another series of functional chairs, called “Sense Seat”, each chair embedded with pressure sensors positioned at key muscular contact points. These sensors provide real-time feedback on posture and sitting habits, gently guiding users toward more comfortable and correct positions.

Furthermore, to me personally, auditory sense is the one that most effectively triggers my sensations and helps me relax. Therefore, I designed multi-sensory experiences: calming ASMR sound, audio visualization projected across all four walls, and a bio-tactile carpet whose varied textures evoke a gentle, embracing touch.

These carefully choreographed sensory stimuli aim to deepen the user’s state of relaxation, creating a therapeutic environment where both the body and mind can feel comfort.
